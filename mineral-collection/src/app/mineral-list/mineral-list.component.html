What is a mineral?

A mineral is a naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical composition and a crystalline structure. Minerals are the building blocks of rocks and are essential to many geological processes.

Minerals can be classified based on their chemical composition, crystal structure, and physical properties. They are typically formed through geological processes such as crystallization from molten rock, precipitation from solution, or alteration of existing minerals.

Minerals are important for various applications, including construction, electronics, and medicine. They are also valuable for scientific research, as they provide insights into the Earth's history and processes.
